Conclusion¶

  • DCT is performed on 'lenna_gray.jpg'.
  • We will take the DCT of the original image is computed and the inverse of the same DCT is calculated, we get the same image back.
  • If last 4 rows and last 4 cols of DCT of the original image are converted to 0, then inverse DCT of the given image shows almost similar quality to the original image.
  • This shows that top 48 out of 64 coefficients can be retained to get acceptable quality of the image.
  • If the last 6 rows and 6 cols are ignored to further compress the image, then the inverse DCT of the given image will show significant distortion.
  • Same results are observed if the given image is 'test.png'.
